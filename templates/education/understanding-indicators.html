Understanding indicators

What are technical indicators?

Traders who adhere to technical analysis rely on heuristic or pattern-based signals known as technical indicators. These signals emerge from factors such as the price, volume, and open interest of securities or contracts.

Utilizing historical data as a foundation, technical analysts harness indicators to anticipate forthcoming price fluctuations. Among the prevalent technical indicators are the Relative Strength Index (RSI), Money Flow Index (MFI), stochastics, moving average convergence divergence (MACD), and Bollinger Bands.

Technical indicators, also known as “technicals,” are focused on historical trading data, such as price, volume, and open interest, rather than the fundamentals of a business, such as earnings, revenue, or profit margins. Technical indicators are commonly used by active traders, since they’re designed to analyze short-term price movements, but long-term investors may also use technical indicators to identify entry and exit points.

Type of indicators

Technical indicators in trading can be classified into two fundamental categories:

Overlays: These indicators share the same scale as prices and are depicted directly over the price data on a stock chart. Examples encompass moving averages and Bollinger Bands, among others.

Oscillators: Indicators falling under this category oscillate between local minimum and maximum points, and they are positioned either above or below a price chart. Noteworthy instances include the stochastic oscillator, MACD, and RSI.

Traders often engage a variety of technical indicators when scrutinizing a security. With a vast array of options available, traders must opt for indicators that align with their strategy and invest effort in comprehending their mechanisms. Traders might also integrate technical indicators with more subjective methods of technical analysis, like interpreting chart patterns, to formulate trade concepts. The quantitative nature of technical indicators also makes them suitable for integration into automated trading systems.
